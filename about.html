About Langcos

Langcos was founded in December 2025 by Will Cerne, a PhD student in theoretical cosmology and early universe physics in Tokyo, Japan.

Meet the Creator

I'm Will Cerne, a graduate student of theoretical cosmology at the Institute of Science Tokyo. For those interested, you can learn more about my professional work by clicking here.

“Langcos” — the language of the cosmos — began as a promise I made to myself during my undergraduate years.

As a physics student, I often found myself accepting powerful ideas without truly understanding them. Why does the Principle of Stationary Action work so universally? How can magnetism depend on velocity if the laws of physics are frame-independent? Why does adding angular momentum reduce to finding irreducible representations of SO(3)? Why do Taylor series converge to functions that aren’t polynomials?

I learned how to solve the problems. I passed the exams. But I knew there was a deeper layer of understanding that I hadn’t yet reached.

It wasn’t until graduate school that many of these ideas finally clicked — not just computationally, but structurally. I began to see how the pieces fit together. I began to think more like a theoretical physicist rather than a student completing assignments.

Langcos was created to bridge that gap.

This project is dedicated to ambitious physics and mathematics students who want more than just exam solutions. Langcos focuses on the structure behind calculations, the logic behind physical principles, and the habits of thought that turn coursework into real understanding.

If you are preparing for graduate school, struggling to connect concepts across courses, or simply want to think more clearly and rigorously about physics, Langcos is built for you.
