About Langcos

A calm, structured approach to learning math and physics.

Mission

Langcos exists to make hard material feel organized and learnable—with compact guides that are rigorous, readable, and focused on the ideas that actually unlock problems.

  • Clarity first: the map comes before the details, so you always know what you’re doing and why.
  • Problem-ready structure: standard methods, common patterns, and the “next step” logic you can reuse on assignments and exams.
  • Pitfalls called out: the small, repeatable mistakes that cost points—explained before they happen.
  • Rigorous, not heavy: enough mathematical honesty to trust, without turning into a textbook.
  • Built to review: concise formatting you can skim quickly and return to when you’re stuck.

Who I am

I'm Will Cerne, a graduate student of theoretical cosmology at the Institute of Science Tokyo. For those interested, you can learn more about my professional work by clicking here.

“Langcos”, meaning "language of the cosmos", is a project of mine to help undergraduates in physics and mathematics understand difficult topics in these majors better. Throughout my ungraduate career, there were certain topics that I did not fully understand until much later into my graduate level studies. Now knowing these things, I believe that the issue is not the complexity of the topic, but rather how it is taught. Professors often don't have time for individual questions, and teaching assistants often lack the ability to clearly explain topics or justify reasonings. Langcos was created to share with the world "the language of the cosmos", in a way that anyone, even you, can understand!

Teaching philosophy

  • Structure beats speed: a good map is better than more content.
  • Examples are the proof: learn the pattern, then generalize.
  • Pitfalls matter: most errors come from a small set of common confusions.
