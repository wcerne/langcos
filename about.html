About Langcos

A calm, structured approach to learning math and physics.

Meet the Creator

I'm Will Cerne, a graduate student of theoretical cosmology at the Institute of Science Tokyo. For those interested, you can learn more about my professional work by clicking here.

“Langcos”, meaning language of the cosmos, is my project to help undergraduates in physics and mathematics learn difficult topics faster—and with less frustration. I wrote each educational study guide with my undergraduate self in mind. Each study guide contains explanations I wish I heard, content I wish was taught, and clarity I wish my instructors had. My hope is that my study guides can help undergraduates who felt the same way that I did.

Each Langcos study guide promises to deliver:

  • Reusable problem-solving patterns: standard methods and “next-step” logic you can apply immediately on assignments and exams.
  • Worked examples that teach strategy: not just the algebra—how to recognize what to do and how to check your result.
  • Common pitfalls called out early: the repeatable mistakes that cost points, explained before they happen.
  • Rigor without the textbook bloat: enough mathematical honesty to trust, written to be readable and efficient.
  • A layout built for review: compact structure you can skim quickly, return to when stuck, and use as a reference.
